Halal heads-up: Most of Chinatown is not halal. Din Tai Fung is not halal-certified but is pork-free on some items — check the menu carefully. Great halal options nearby: Halal Guys, Mamouns Falafel, or explore the halal food carts around Midtown.

Food
1633 Broadway, Midtown Manhattan
World-famous Taiwanese soup dumplings (XLB). Try the chicken dumplings & chocolate XLB for dessert. Get on the waitlist early — gets very packed.

254 E 60th St, Upper East Side
An aerial cable car right over the East River. Do this at night — the city lights are magical. Same price as the subway (~$2.90) with your MetroCard.

AUTOPHOTO Gallery
Activity
121 Orchard St, Lower East Side
Vintage photobooth museum with 8+ working booths. Free entry, $8–12 per booth. 5-star rated — the staff narrate the history of each booth. A must if you love photobooths. Closed Mondays.

NYC Ferry Ride
Activity
Multiple piers, East River & Hudson
Only $4.50 — best value view in the city. Take it at dusk to see One World Trade lit up across the water. East River route is the most scenic. Book nothing, just show up.
★ 4.6 · $4.50 flat

SUMMIT One Vanderbilt
Activity
45 E 42nd St, Midtown
Immersive observation experience: glass floors, balloon room, mirror infinity rooms. Book early AM for empty photo ops (8–9am slot). Open until midnight most nights. Requires tickets.

The Edge — Hudson Yards
Activity
30 Hudson Yards, West Side
Highest outdoor sky deck in NYC — glass floor, 360° views, Hudson River on one side & the whole skyline on the other. Sunset slot is the move. Buy tickets online to skip the line.
★ 4.6 · Outdoor deck

Brooklyn Bridge
Sightsee
Connecting Manhattan & Brooklyn
Walk the 1.3-mile pedestrian path for iconic skyline views. Go early morning for fewer crowds. Pair with a DUMBO walk right after — Washington St. gives the best framed shot of the bridge.
★ 4.8 · Free · 30–45 min walk

DUMBO, Brooklyn
Sightsee
Washington St & Water St, Brooklyn
One of NYC's most photogenic neighbourhoods. The cobblestone streets frame the bridge perfectly. Washington St is the shot everyone comes for. Great cafés and waterfront views nearby.
★ 4.8 · Free · Pair with Bridge walk

Canal Street
Sightsee
Canal St, Chinatown / SoHo border
The chaotic, sensory-overload heart of Chinatown. Knock-off bags, cheap phone cases, bubble tea, and street food vendors. Bring cash — many vendors are cash only. Fun to wander, not really a destination.
★ 4.5 · Free · Bring cash
